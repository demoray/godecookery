• Jakke of Dover A sort of meat or fish pie; the meaning is not certain. Chaucer describes a stale or reheated pasty, dressed in blood or gravy to make it appear fresh.

    • "And many a Jakke of Dovere hastow soold

    That hath been twies hoot and twies coold" - The Cook's Prologue

    This is one of the unappetizing food items that Chuacer's Canterbury Tales Cook sells from his shop; apparantly they maintain quite a lengthy shelf life before reaching their unlucky customers.

  • Jubbe A jug, a vessel. In a 14th century account a jubbe held 4 gallons, but there were probably other sizes.

    • "With breed and chese, and good ale in a jubbe" - The Miller's Tale

    "With hym broghte he a jubbe of malvesye" - The Shipman's Tale
